Tennessee’s 7th Congressional District

Each state elects two senators to the United States Senate for staggered 6-year terms. Senators represent the entire state. Tennessee’s senators are:

Sen. Lamar Alexander

Senator for Tennessee

Alexander is the senior senator from Tennessee and is Republican. He has served since Jan 7, 2003. Alexander is next up for reelection in 2020.

Commitee Membership

Lamar Alexander sits on the following committees:

  • Ex Officio, Subcommittee on Children and Families
  • Ex Officio, Subcommittee on Employment and Workplace Safety
  • Ex Officio, Subcommittee on Primary Health and Retirement Security
  • Chair, Subcommittee on Energy and Water Development
  • Member, Subcommittee on Commerce, Justice, Science, and Related Agencies
  • Member, Subcommittee on Department of Defense
  • Member, Subcommittee on Department of the Interior, Environment, and Related Agencies
  • Member, Subcommittee on Departments of Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education, and Related Agencies
  • Member, Subcommittee on Transportation, Housing and Urban Development, and Related Agencies

Enacted Legislation

Alexander was the primary sponsor of 14 bills that were enacted. The most recent include:

  • S. 1866: Hurricanes Harvey, Irma, and Maria Education Relief Act of 2017
  • S. 1609: Energy and Water Development and Related Agencies Appropriations Act, 2018
